# Anchor Marianas — go-to-market narrative

_How the proprietary product reaches the market. Written to read as one repeatable, product-led motion, because the MTEP New track scores capability to execute, not revenue._

## The honesty line (hold it everywhere)
Anchor is pre-revenue on the AI-services line, with no paying AI clients yet. That is fine on this track. What must be shown is that demand is real and the motion is repeatable, without inflating a single number. A signed agreement is labeled signed; a conversation is labeled a conversation; Hilton is a delivered web product and reference, not an AI client; chovin is a peer endorsement.

## The motion (one product-led funnel)
1. **Wedge, free:** run AnchorScan on a target business (and offer a free one-page site to those that lack one). Every relationship starts with demonstrated value, not a pitch. This is what drives acquisition cost toward zero, and it is only possible because the diagnostic is a real product.
2. **Land, low monthly:** the owner sees a real problem in their own reviews and buys the fix. Website Care and monitoring at a low monthly rate is the recurring base.
3. **Expand, the real money:** the highest-value fix is a 24/7 multilingual AI receptionist, shipped in about a week for a setup fee plus a monthly retainer. This is distribution and revenue for the product, never the company's identity.
4. **Beachhead, narrow on purpose:** one segment first, Pacific and Malaysian hospitality and food SMBs of 5 to 100 staff, so the funnel looks focused and provably scalable rather than scattershot.
