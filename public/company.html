01 · The product

AnchorScan

Small businesses are surrounded by signals they cannot read: reviews that name the same complaint every week, a site that quietly turns customers away, calls that go unanswered. The AI tools to fix this exist, but an owner running a restaurant or a repair shop cannot wield them. AnchorScan is the layer in between. It ingests those public signals and returns a diagnosis in the owner's language, not a dashboard, and not a pitch.

Find the pattern

A language model surfaces the recurring operational problems, evidence-backed, with no invented numbers.

03 / RETURN

03 · Market and why now

The underserved majority

Small and medium businesses are the backbone of the Asia-Pacific economy and the least served by AI. The frontier models are aimed at engineers and enterprises. The owner-operator, a hotelier, a clinic, a trades business, is left out, even though they generate the reviews, the calls, and the demand that AI is best at reading. Anchor's wedge is meeting that owner where they already are: their public footprint, diagnosed for free, in their own words.

Why now

Two things arrived at once: models good enough to read messy real-world business signals reliably, and an SMB base finally aware they are behind. The gap is not capability, it is a translation layer built for people who do not code. That layer is a product, and it did not exist for this segment. AnchorScan is Anchor building it.

04 · Why Malaysia

Malaysia as the ASEAN base

Anchor's motion was proven on Guam, a small, English-speaking, SMB-dense Pacific market. Malaysia is the same problem at a scale that matters: a large, digitally-maturing SMB economy, multilingual in exactly the languages the Pacific and ASEAN share, and the natural gateway into the wider ASEAN market Anchor is built to serve. The Malaysia Digital ecosystem (MDEC, the Digital Hubs, the tech-entrepreneur pathway) is designed to host a company like this, and the free-diagnostic-then-productize motion ports directly to Malaysian hospitality, food, and services SMBs.

05 · How the product reaches the market

One product-led motion

Anchor does not sell consulting. It uses a free version of its own product to earn the relationship, then monetizes the fix. The motion is deliberately narrow so it looks focused and repeatable, not scattershot.

  • Wedge: a free AnchorScan (and a free one-page site for businesses that lack one) starts every relationship with demonstrated value, not a pitch. This lowers acquisition cost to near zero.
  • Land: the owner sees a real problem in their own reviews, then buys the fix. Care and monitoring at a low monthly rate is the recurring base.
  • Expand: the highest-value fix, a 24/7 multilingual AI receptionist, ships in about a week for a setup fee plus a monthly retainer. This is distribution and revenue for the product, not the company's identity.
  • Beachhead: one segment first, Pacific and Malaysian hospitality and food SMBs of 5 to 100 staff, so the funnel math is legible and the motion is provably scalable.
